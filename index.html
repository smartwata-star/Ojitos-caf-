The story

It started with a cold brew,
a hand-drawn logo, and a folding table.

Ojitos means little eyes — what her abuela called her as a kid, watching everything, always curious. In early 2025 that same curiosity turned into a cart: one cooler, one espresso setup, and a notebook of recipes built around the flavors she grew up on — cafecito de olla, mazapán, horchata — pushed somewhere new.

There's no storefront. No fixed hours. Just a location dropped on Instagram stories each morning, a hedge-lined curb in the 91731, and a line that starts before the first cup is poured. Every drink is finished to order: the cold foam piped last, the crumble pressed in by hand, the sticker turned label-out before it goes in your hands.

— that's the whole menu, made with little eyes wide open.
